Tamasha (2015) - Movie Review

Director : Imtiaz Ali

Music : A R Rahman

Cast : Ranbir Kapoor, Deepika Padukone

Summary

Tamasha is a story about Ved (Ranbir Kapoor), a man trapped in the monotony of societal expectations, and Tara (Deepika Padukone), who sees his true, free-spirited self. They meet in Corsica and decide to enjoy their time together without revealing their real identities. Years later, Tara finds Ved in Delhi, only to realize that he is nothing like the lively man she met earlier. The film then follows Ved’s struggle to break free from the routine life imposed by society and rediscover his passion for storytelling.

Performance

  • Ranbir Kapoor delivers one of his career-best performances, portraying Ved’s inner turmoil with depth and authenticity. His transition from a carefree storyteller to a robotic corporate worker is heartbreaking yet powerful.
  • Deepika Padukone shines as Tara, bringing warmth and emotion to the film. Her chemistry with Ranbir is undeniable, making their moments together deeply moving.

Direction & Screenplay

Imtiaz Ali crafts a deeply philosophical narrative, exploring themes of identity, self-discovery, and breaking societal molds. The non-linear storytelling keeps the audience engaged, although the complexity might not appeal to everyone.

Music

A.R. Rahman’s soundtrack is soulful and blends seamlessly with the film's themes. Songs like Agar Tum Saath Ho and Matargashti elevate the emotions of the film, making the music a character in itself.

Cinematography

The movie is visually stunning, from the vibrant landscapes of Corsica to the mundane corporate world of Delhi. The contrasting visuals effectively highlight Ved’s internal conflict.

Final Verdict

Tamasha is not a typical Bollywood love story. It’s an introspective, layered film that resonates with those who have ever felt lost in societal expectations. While the narrative might feel slow or abstract at times, it leaves a lasting impact with its emotional depth and strong performances.
