Hi Nanna (2023) - Movie Review

Director : Shouryuv

Genre : Romance, Drama, Family

Cast : Nani, Mrunal Thakur, Baby Kiara Khanna, Priyadarshi

Summary

Hi Nanna is an emotional drama centered around Viraj (Nani), a single father who shares a beautiful bond with his young daughter, Mahi (Kiara Khanna). Their peaceful life takes an unexpected turn when Yashna (Mrunal Thakur) enters their world, bringing in new emotions, hidden truths, and a journey of healing. As the story unfolds, secrets from the past are revealed, testing relationships and redefining the meaning of love, forgiveness, and second chances.

Performance

  • Nani delivers one of his most mature and heartfelt performances, portraying the struggles and emotions of a single father with incredible depth. His chemistry with Baby Kiara Khanna is the heart of the film.
  • Mrunal Thakur impresses yet again with her elegance and strong screen presence. Her role adds emotional weight to the story, making it more than just a simple family drama
  • Kiara Khanna, as Mahi, steals the show with her innocence and natural performance, making every emotional scene even more impactful.

Direction & Screenplay

Debut director Shouryuv crafts a beautifully layered emotional narrative that keeps the audience engaged. The storytelling is simple yet deeply moving, balancing emotions, romance, and drama without making it overly melodramatic.

Music

Hesham Abdul Wahab’s music is a soulful highlight of the film. Songs like Samayama and Gaaju Bomma perfectly blend with the story, enhancing the emotions on screen.

Cinematography

The cinematography beautifully captures the warmth of a father-daughter bond, with soft tones and heartwarming visuals. His visual storytelling, backed by soothing cinematography, enhances the film’s emotional depth.

Final Verdict

A beautifully crafted emotional drama with heartfelt performances, soothing music, and a touching storyline. Hi Nanna tugs at your heartstrings and reminds you of the power of love and relationships. A must-watch for those who love family-oriented, feel-good cinema!
